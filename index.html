The Artist

Laurie Werth

"I paint the way a garden grows — with a plan that the weather and the soil will always revise."

Trained at the Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts, Laurie works in ink, watercolor, and mineral salt on archival paper. Her process balances deliberate mark-making with the spontaneous chemistry of water, pigment, and salt crystals pulling color into unexpected organic forms.

Based in the Brandywine Valley, her work draws from native botanicals, pollinator gardens, and the wildlife that passes through the backyard studio. Each piece captures a moment where control and chance meet — the ink line dissolving at its edges, the salt bloom that becomes a flower petal, the watercolor wash that settles into its own quiet landscape.

How Each Print Begins

The Process

🖋
01

Ink Line

Each piece starts with archival ink — bold structural lines that will anchor the composition as layers build.

🧂
02

Salt Texture

Mineral salt crystals are scattered into wet washes. As the water evaporates, the salt pulls pigment into organic, unrepeatable patterns.

💧
03

Watercolor Layers

Transparent color is built up slowly — wet-on-wet for soft blooms, dry brush for detail — allowing each layer to breathe.

🖨
04

Archival Print

Finished originals are scanned at high resolution and printed with pigment-based giclée inks on fine art paper — rated for 100+ years of color life.
